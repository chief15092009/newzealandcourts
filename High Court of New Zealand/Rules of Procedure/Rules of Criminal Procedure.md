# HIGH COURT RULES OF CRIMINAL PROCEDURE

---

## 1. Criminal Information

### Rule 1.1 — Commencement of Proceedings
A **Criminal Information** is the originating pleading by which a criminal proceeding is commenced in the High Court. It shall be the first pleading filed by **Crown Counsel** in every criminal action.

### Rule 1.2 — Contents of a Criminal Information
A Criminal Information shall contain:
- The full legal name of the **Defendant**;
- A complete list of charges laid;
- A concise description of the factual occurrence supporting each charge;
- The statutory or legal basis of each charge;
- The date of filing; and
- The signature of Crown Counsel representing the prosecution.

### Rule 1.3 — Time for Filing
A Criminal Information must be filed within **48 hours** of being directed by the Court, unless otherwise ordered.

### Rule 1.4 — Probable Cause Requirement
The Crown must establish **probable cause** that an offence has been committed by the defendant in order for a Criminal Information to be accepted.

### Rule 1.4.1 — Preliminary Determination
Notwithstanding Rule 1.4, the Court may elect to accept a Criminal Information provisionally and convene a **preliminary hearing** to determine probable cause.

### Rule 1.5 — Notice of Appearance
Upon acceptance of a Criminal Information, the Court shall issue a **Notice of Appearance** compelling the defendant to appear within **72 hours**, unless otherwise ordered.

---

## 2. Right to Counsel and Court-Appointed Representation

### Rule 2.1 — Right to Counsel
Every defendant has the right to adequate legal representation in criminal proceedings.

### Rule 2.2 — Duty Counsel
Where a defendant cannot afford counsel or appears unrepresented, the Court shall appoint **duty counsel** to represent the defendant.

### Rule 2.3 — Waiver of Counsel
A defendant may waive the right to counsel, provided the waiver is made knowingly, voluntarily, and on the record.

---

## 3. Preliminary Hearing and Arraignment

### Rule 3.1 — Arraignment
Prior to trial scheduling, the defendant shall be arraigned and enter a plea to each charge listed in the Criminal Information.

### Rule 3.2 — Default Plea
If a defendant fails to enter a plea within the time directed by the Court, a plea of **not guilty** shall be entered by default.

### Rule 3.3 — Guilty Pleas
Counsel for the defence shall not enter a plea of guilty without the express consent of the defendant.

### Rule 3.4 — Preliminary Motions
Preliminary motions may be filed by either party up to **24 hours** prior to the scheduled trial time, unless otherwise permitted.

### Rule 3.5 — Preliminary Hearing
Where a Criminal Information is accepted on an expedited basis, the Court shall conduct a preliminary hearing to determine probable cause.

### Rule 3.5.1 — Dismissal
If the Court finds no probable cause, the proceeding shall be dismissed and the defendant released without conditions.

---

## 4. Pleas and Plea Agreements

### Rule 4.1 — Plea Agreements
Plea agreements must be negotiated between Crown Counsel and the defendant and approved by the Court.

### Rule 4.2 — Contents of a Plea Agreement
A plea agreement shall include:
- Charges withdrawn by the Crown;
- Charges to which the defendant pleads guilty;
- Charges to which the defendant pleads not guilty;
- Agreed facts and sentencing recommendations, if any;
- The date the agreement was entered into; and
- The signatures of the Crown and the defendant.

### Rule 4.3 — Filing Format
All plea agreements must be submitted in **PDF format**.

---

## 5. Pretrial Motions and Pleadings

### Rule 5.1 — Permitted Motions
The following motions may be filed:
- Motion to dismiss (with or without prejudice);
- Motion to suppress evidence;
- Motion for discovery;
- Motion for change of venue;
- Motion to recuse;
- Motion for directed verdict;
- Motion to compel;
- Criminal Information;
- Amended Criminal Information.

### Rule 5.1.1 — Additional Motions
The Court may permit additional motions where justice requires.

### Rule 5.2 — Filing Deadlines
All motions and responses must be filed within **48 hours** of Court direction, unless otherwise ordered.

---

## 6. Discovery and Disclosure

### Rule 6.1 — Discovery Period
The Court may order a discovery period of no less than **24 hours**, unless agreed otherwise.

### Rule 6.2 — Crown Disclosure Obligations
Crown Counsel shall disclose **all evidence** in its possession, whether inculpatory or exculpatory.

### Rule 6.3 — Labelling
All evidence must be clearly labelled and organised.

### Rule 6.4 — Late Evidence
Evidence or witnesses not disclosed within the discovery period shall not be admitted without leave of the Court.

### Rule 6.5 — Discovery Filing
Discovery materials must be filed within **48 hours** of Court direction.

### Rule 6.6 — Supplementary Discovery
Further discovery requests must be fulfilled within **48 hours**, unless otherwise ordered.

---

## 7. Formatting Requirements

### Rule 7.1 — Required Elements
All documents must contain:
- The defendant’s name;
- Title of the document;
- Date of filing; and
- Signature of the filing party.

### Rule 7.2 — Templates
Court-approved templates shall be used where available.

### Rule 7.3 — File Format
All documents must be submitted in **PDF format**.

---

## 8. Affidavits and Depositions

### Rule 8.1 — Use
Affidavits and depositions may be used where witness statements are required.

### Rule 8.2 — Oath Requirement
All affidavits and depositions must be sworn under oath and signed by the witness.

### Rule 8.3 — Evidentiary Status
Affidavits and depositions shall be treated as evidence.

### Rule 8.4 — Presumption of Validity
Depositions taken before a judge and returned by a proper officer shall be presumed valid unless proven otherwise.

---

## 9. Objections and Defences

### Rule 9.1 — Raising Objections
Either party may raise objections during trial.

### Rule 9.2 — Timing
Objections may be raised during:
- Opening statements;
- Closing statements;
- Evidence presentation; or
- Witness examination.

### Rule 9.3 — Response
The Court may permit a response to an objection.

### Rule 9.4 — Sustained Objections
Sustained objections shall result in the struck material being removed from the record unless otherwise ordered.

---

## 10. Subpoenas and Witness Summons

### Rule 10.1 — Issuance
Subpoenas may be issued to:
- Produce documents or evidence; or
- Compel witness attendance.

### Rule 10.2 — Self-Incrimination
A defendant may not be compelled to testify.

### Rule 10.3 — Witness Rights
Witnesses must testify or provide affidavits but retain the right against self-incrimination.

---

## 11. Trial Procedure

### Rule 11.1 — Commencement
Trial commences when the Court is formally called to order.

### Rule 11.2 — Order of Trial
- Opening statements (Crown first);
- Crown’s evidence;
- Defence cross-examination;
- Crown re-examination;
- Crown rests;
- Defence evidence;
- Crown cross-examination;
- Defence re-examination;
- Closing submissions (Crown first).

---

## 12. Verdict and Sentencing

### Rule 12.1 — Determination
The Court shall determine guilt or innocence by judgment or order.

### Rule 12.2 — Written Judgments
Written judgments shall include:
- The verdict;
- Findings of fact;
- Legal reasoning;
- Sentence or fine imposed;
- Date of issue; and
- Judicial signature.
