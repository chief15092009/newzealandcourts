# HIGH COURT RULES OF CIVIL PROCEDURE

---

## 1. Originating Proceedings and Statements of Defence

### Rule 1.1 — Originating Document
A **Notice of Claim** is the originating pleading by which a civil proceeding is commenced in the High Court.

### Rule 1.2 — Contents of a Notice of Claim
A Notice of Claim shall contain:
- The full legal names of the **Claimant(s)**;
- The full legal names of the **Defendant(s)**;
- A concise statement of material facts;
- Clearly pleaded allegations, divided into numbered counts where appropriate;
- A clear identification of each cause of action relied upon;
- The relief or remedies sought;
- The date of filing; and
- The signature of the filing party or their legal representative.

### Rule 1.2.1 — Insufficient Pleadings
Where a Notice of Claim fails to disclose an intelligible cause of action or sufficient factual basis, the Court may dismiss the claim in whole or in part.

### Rule 1.3 — Form of Pleadings
All Notices of Claim must:
- Be clearly headed;
- Use numbered paragraphs; and
- Be drafted in a logical and readable manner.

### Rule 1.4 — Amended Pleadings
A Claimant may amend a Notice of Claim with leave of the Court, or as directed by the Court.

### Rule 1.5 — Statement of Defence (Reply)
A **Reply** is the pleading filed by a Defendant in response to a Notice of Claim.

### Rule 1.6 — Contents of a Reply
A Reply shall contain:
- The parties to the proceeding;
- A response to each allegation pleaded;
- Admissions, denials, or statements of non-admission;
- All affirmative defences relied upon;
- Any relief sought by the Defendant;
- The date of filing; and
- The signature of the filing party or their legal representative.

### Rule 1.7 — Time for Filing
Notices of Claim and Replies shall be filed within **48 hours** of being directed by the Court, unless otherwise ordered.

---

## 2. Permitted Pleadings

### Rule 2.1 — Limitation on Pleadings
No pleadings shall be permitted except:
- A Notice of Claim;
- A Reply;
- An amended Notice of Claim; and
- An amended Reply,

unless leave is granted by the Court.

### Rule 2.1.1 — Special Pleadings
The Court may permit special or additional pleadings where justice requires.

### Rule 2.2 — Filing Deadlines
All pleadings under this Part must be filed within **48 hours** of Court direction, unless otherwise ordered.

---

## 3. Motions and Interlocutory Applications

### Rule 3.1 — Motions Generally
A party may apply to the Court by motion for any order relating to the proceedings.

### Rule 3.2 — Permitted Motions
The following motions are permitted:
- Dismissal with prejudice;
- Dismissal without prejudice;
- Summary judgment;
- Directed verdict;
- New trial;
- Motion to compel.

### Rule 3.2.1 — Other Motions
The Court may permit additional motions where good cause is shown.

### Rule 3.3 — Contents of a Motion
A motion shall include:
- The parties;
- The title of the motion;
- The legal and factual grounds relied upon;
- The date of filing; and
- The signature of the filing party or their representative.

### Rule 3.4 — Hearings on Motions
The Court may convene a hearing on its own motion or upon application by a party.

### Rule 3.5 — Filing Timeframes
Motions and responses shall be filed within **48 hours** of Court direction unless otherwise ordered.

---

## 4. Discovery

### Rule 4.1 — Disclosure Obligations
Each party shall disclose:
- Evidence intended to be relied upon;
- Witnesses intended to be called; and
- Any additional material ordered by the Court.

### Rule 4.2 — Initial Discovery Deadline
Discovery materials must be filed within **72 hours** of Court direction.

### Rule 4.3 — Further Discovery
Supplementary discovery shall be completed within **48 hours** of direction.

---

## 5. Jurisdictional Classification of Cases

### Rule 5.1 — Civil Proceedings
All claims within the High Court’s jurisdiction shall be treated as civil proceedings.

### Rule 5.2 — Declaratory and Injunctive Relief
Applications for declaratory or injunctive relief fall within the civil jurisdiction of the Court.

### Rule 5.3 — Expungement Applications
Applications seeking to seal or expunge records shall be governed by Part 13 of these Rules.

### Rule 5.3.1 — Convictions
Applications seeking to seal or expunge convictions shall be dismissed.

---

## 6. Court–Party Communications

### Rule 6.1 — Designated Communication Channels
The Court shall designate an official communication channel, including Discord where appropriate.

### Rule 6.2 — Conduct
The Court retains full authority to regulate conduct and sanction disruptions.

### Rule 6.3 — Attendance
All counsel of record shall be present within designated communication channels.

### Rule 6.4 — Scheduling
Hearings and trials shall be scheduled through the designated channel.

### Rule 6.5 — Filing
Motions and submissions may be received through the designated channel as directed.

---

## 7. Third Parties and Witnesses

### Rule 7.1 — Orders to Compel
The Court may compel compliance with lawful orders.

### Rule 7.2 — Witnesses
Witnesses may testify with leave of the Court.

### Rule 7.3 — Intervenors
Third parties may participate where permitted by the Court.

---

## 8. Service of Documents

### Rule 8.1 — Service Requirement
All filed documents must be served on affected parties.

### Rule 8.2 — Substituted Service
Where service cannot be effected, the Court may direct alternative service.

### Rule 8.3 — Service by Publication
Service by publication may be recognised where reasonable efforts have failed.

---

## 9. Formatting Requirements

### Rule 9.1 — File Format
All documents must be submitted in **PDF format**.

### Rule 9.2 — Compliance
All filings must comply with Court-issued formatting directions.

---

## 10. Notices of Appearance

### Rule 10.1 — Issuance
The Court may issue a Notice of Appearance requiring attendance.

### Rule 10.2 — Service
Notices must be served at least **48 hours** prior to the required appearance.

---

## 11. Trial Procedure

### Rule 11.1 — Order of Proceedings
- Opening submissions (Claimant first);
- Claimant’s evidence;
- Cross-examination;
- Re-examination;
- Defence evidence;
- Closing submissions (Claimant first).

### Rule 11.2 — Oral Motions
The Court may entertain oral motions at any stage.

---

## 12. Judgments

### Rule 12.1 — Determination
The Court shall determine proceedings by judgment or order.

### Rule 12.2 — Written Judgments
Judgments should include:
- The decision;
- Findings of fact;
- Legal reasoning;
- Orders made;
- Date; and
- Judicial signature.

---

## 13. Expungement Proceedings

### Rule 13.1
Expungement applications are governed exclusively by this Part.

### Rule 13.2
No tort claims may be advanced in expungement proceedings.

### Rule 13.3–13.10
The remaining provisions of this Part apply **mutatis mutandis** to proceedings before the High Court.
