`PURPOSE OF THE COURTS & JURISDICTION`

<p>
The Supreme Court of New Zealand is the final appellate court and the highest judicial authority within the New Zealand court hierarchy. It exercises jurisdiction over appeals from the High Court of New Zealand and, where permitted by law, matters of exceptional public or legal importance.
</p>

<p>
The Court is responsible for the authoritative determination of questions of law, the development and clarification of legal principles, and the maintenance of consistency and certainty within the justice system.
