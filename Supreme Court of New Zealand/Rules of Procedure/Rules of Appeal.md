# RULES OF APPEAL  
---

## 1. Application of These Rules

**Rule 1.1.** These Rules govern the filing, processing, administration, and management of all appeals lodged with the Supreme Court.

**Rule 1.2.** These Rules apply to:
- Applications for leave to appeal;
- Notices of Appeal;
- Cross-appeals;
- Interlocutory appeals where permitted; and
- Any ancillary filings required for an appeal to be placed before the Court.

**Rule 1.3.** These Rules do not govern:
- The admissibility of evidence;
- Standards of review;
- Substantive legal argument; or
- Trial-level procedures.

---

## 2. Commencement of an Appeal

**Rule 2.1.** An appeal is commenced only upon the proper filing of a Notice of Appeal or Application for Leave to Appeal through the Electronic Registry.

**Rule 2.2.** An appeal is not deemed filed until:
- All required documents are submitted;
- All mandatory fields are completed; and
- The filing is accepted by the Registry.

**Rule 2.3.** An improperly filed appeal has no legal effect.

---

## 3. Application for Leave to Appeal

**Rule 3.1.** Where leave to appeal is required, the appellant must file an Application for Leave to Appeal before filing a Notice of Appeal.

**Rule 3.2.** An Application for Leave to Appeal shall include:
- The full case name and originating court;
- The decision or order sought to be appealed;
- The date the decision was issued;
- A concise statement of the questions proposed for appeal;
- The relief sought; and
- The name and signature of the filing party or counsel.

**Rule 3.3.** The Application must be filed within the timeframe prescribed by the Court or registry notice.

**Rule 3.4.** If leave is denied, the appeal shall be closed administratively.

---

## 4. Notice of Appeal

**Rule 4.1.** A Notice of Appeal must be filed after leave is granted or where leave is not required.

**Rule 4.2.** The Notice of Appeal shall contain:
- The full style of cause;
- The registry file number of the originating case;
- The court and judge whose decision is appealed;
- The date of the decision;
- The orders appealed from;
- The relief sought on appeal; and
- The signature of the appellant or counsel.

**Rule 4.3.** Only orders expressly identified in the Notice of Appeal are before the Court.

---

## 5. Filing Deadlines

**Rule 5.1.** Filing deadlines are strictly enforced.

**Rule 5.2.** The Registry may reject filings submitted outside prescribed time limits.

**Rule 5.3.** An extension of time may be requested by formal motion, stating:
- The length of extension sought; and
- The reason for delay.

**Rule 5.4.** Failure to meet deadlines without leave may result in dismissal.

---

## 6. Record Preparation and Filing

**Rule 6.1.** The appellant is responsible for preparing and filing the appeal record.

**Rule 6.2.** The record shall include:
- The decision appealed from;
- The notice of appeal;
- Relevant procedural orders; and
- Any materials required by registry direction.

**Rule 6.3.** Records must be indexed, paginated, and clearly labeled.

**Rule 6.4.** The Registry may reject incomplete or improperly formatted records.

---

## 7. Service of Documents

**Rule 7.1.** All appeal documents must be served on all parties of record.

**Rule 7.2.** Proof of service must accompany every filed document.

**Rule 7.3.** Service shall be effected:
- Electronically through the registry; or
- By another method approved by the Court.

---

## 8. Cross-Appeals

**Rule 8.1.** A respondent wishing to challenge any part of the decision must file a Cross-Appeal.

**Rule 8.2.** A Cross-Appeal shall comply with the same filing requirements as a Notice of Appeal.

**Rule 8.3.** Failure to file a Cross-Appeal bars the respondent from seeking variation of the decision.

---

## 9. Motions in an Appeal

**Rule 9.1.** Procedural motions may be filed during an appeal, including:
- Motions to extend time;
- Motions to amend filings;
- Motions to strike filings; and
- Motions to discontinue an appeal.

**Rule 9.2.** All motions must clearly state the relief sought and be filed through the Registry.

---

## 10. Registry Review and Deficiencies

**Rule 10.1.** The Registry shall review filings for compliance with these Rules.

**Rule 10.2.** Where deficiencies are identified, the Registry may:
- Reject the filing;
- Issue a deficiency notice; or
- Require correction within a specified time.

**Rule 10.3.** Failure to correct deficiencies may result in dismissal.

---

## 11. Withdrawal or Abandonment of Appeal

**Rule 11.1.** An appellant may withdraw an appeal by filing a Notice of Discontinuance.

**Rule 11.2.** An appeal may be deemed abandoned if required filings are not completed.

---

## 12. Electronic Filing Requirements

**Rule 12.1.** All appeal documents must be filed electronically.

**Rule 12.2.** Documents must:
- Be in Portable Document Format (PDF);
- Be legible and searchable; and
- Use Court-approved templates where available.

**Rule 12.3.** Altered or falsified documents constitute a serious offence.

---

## 13. Registry Authority

**Rule 13.1.** The Registry may issue administrative directions to ensure orderly processing.

**Rule 13.2.** Registry directions are binding unless set aside by a Judge.

---

## 14. Closing of Appeal

**Rule 14.1.** An appeal is closed when:
- A final judgment is issued; or
- The appeal is dismissed, withdrawn, or abandoned.

**Rule 14.2.** Closed appeals may not be reopened without leave of the Court.
