# RULES OF EVIDENCE (APPEALS)

---

## 1. Rulings on Evidence

**Rule 1.1.** A party may claim error in a ruling to admit or exclude evidence only if the error affects the rights of that party.

**Rule 1.2.** If the ruling admits evidence, a party must object in a timely manner or move to strike, stating the specific ground.

**Rule 1.3.** If the ruling excludes evidence, a party must inform the Court by an offer of proof.

---

## 2. Preliminary Questions

**Rule 2.1.** The Court must decide the following preliminary questions:
- Whether a witness is qualified;
- Whether a privilege exists; and
- Whether evidence is admissible.

**Rule 2.2.** When the relevance of evidence depends on whether a fact exists, proof must be introduced sufficient to support a finding that the fact does exist.

**Rule 2.2.1.** The Court may admit the proposed evidence on the condition that the requirements of Rule 2.2 are fulfilled later.

**Rule 2.2.2.** Any evidence admitted under Rule 2.2.1 may be excluded if the Court determines the requirements of Rule 2.2 were not fulfilled in a timely manner.

---

## 3. Excluding Evidence

**Rule 3.1.** The Court shall exclude evidence if it concludes that:
- A party’s rights or freedoms were unjustifiably limited or denied;
- The evidence was obtained in a manner that unjustifiably limited or denied a guaranteed right or freedom; or
- Having regard to all the circumstances, admission of the evidence would bring the administration of justice into disrepute.

---

## 4. Relevance

**Rule 4.1.** Evidence is relevant if it has any tendency to make a fact more or less probable than it would be without the evidence, and the fact is of consequence in determining the action.

---

## 5. Privilege

**Rule 5.1.** A waiver of privilege extends to undisclosed communications or information if:
- The waiver is intentional; and
- The disclosed and undisclosed communications or information concern the same subject matter.

**Rule 5.2.** Disclosure does not operate as a waiver of privilege if:
- The disclosure was inadvertent;
- Reasonable steps were taken to prevent disclosure; and
- Reasonable steps were promptly taken to rectify the error.

---

## 6. Subpoenas

**Rule 6.1.** Subpoenas may be issued to compel testimony upon request by a party or by the Court *sua sponte* where the evidence sought is relevant and material.

**Rule 6.2.** Subpoenas for the production of documents shall be issued only upon written motion specifying, as precisely as possible, the documents sought.

**Rule 6.3.** Subpoenas shall be served by a clerk or a peace officer.

**Rule 6.4.** Subpoenas must be served no later than 48 hours before the appearance required.

---

## 7. Witnesses

**Rule 7.1.** Every person is competent to be a witness unless proven otherwise.

**Rule 7.2.** A witness may testify only if evidence supports a finding that the witness has personal knowledge of the matter.

**Rule 7.3.** Before testifying, a witness must give an oath or affirmation to testify truthfully.

**Rule 7.4.** A person is not incompetent to give evidence by reason of interest or prior conviction.

**Rule 7.5.** A witness may give evidence identifying an accused through visual or other sensory perception.

**Rule 7.6.** No more than two expert witnesses may be called on either side without leave of the Court.

### Impeaching Witnesses

**Rule 7.7.** A party may not impeach its own witness by general evidence of bad character unless the witness proves adverse, in which case the party may, with leave of the Court, introduce inconsistent statements.

**Rule 7.7.1.** The Court may allow cross-examination on prior inconsistent statements without proof that the witness is adverse.

**Rule 7.7.2.** A witness may be cross-examined on prior recorded statements without the document or recording being shown, subject to the Court’s discretion.

**Rule 7.7.3.** If a witness does not admit making a prior inconsistent statement, proof may be given after the witness’s attention is drawn to the circumstances of the statement.

### Prior Convictions

**Rule 7.8.** A witness may be questioned about prior convictions, excluding traffic or municipal bylaw offences unless entered after trial.

**Rule 7.8.1.** If denied or refused, the conviction may be proven by the opposing party.

**Rule 7.8.2.** Convictions may be proven by official court records, excluding informal digital records.

---

## 8. Affidavit Filing

**Rule 8.1.** Affidavits must be submitted in Portable Document Format (PDF) for registration.

**Rule 8.2.** All affidavits and depositions must be signed and sworn under oath or affirmation.

**Rule 8.3.** Affidavits and depositions are treated as articles of evidence.

---

## 9. Hearsay

**Rule 9.1.** Hearsay—statements made outside the proceeding and offered for their truth—is inadmissible.

**Rule 9.2.** The Court may permit hearsay exceptions, including:
- Admissions by a defendant;
- Statements against interest;
- Statements made in the course of duty;
- Government documents or certified copies;
- Dying declarations;
- Statements made under shock or surprise;
- Sworn testimony from a previous proceeding;
- Recorded past recollections;
- Statements of state of mind or present intention;
- Statutory exceptions;
- Statements forming part of the narrative; and
- Evidence that is both necessary and reliable.

---

## 10. Incriminating Questions

**Rule 10.1.** A witness shall not be excused from answering a question on the ground that it may incriminate them or establish civil liability.

**Rule 10.2.** Answers compelled under Rule 10.1 shall not be admissible against the witness in criminal proceedings, except for perjury or contradictory evidence.
